Roti Canai

Ingredients for Roti Canai

  • 1 kilo plain flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tsp salt
  • 4 tbsp condensed milk
  • 1 egg
  • 500ml water
  • 2 tbsp margarine

Ingredients for Dalcha Curry

  • 200g / 1 cup Mung Dal
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Sufficient water (for boiling)
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Chicken bones
  • 1/2 bottle gourd
  • 1/2 long eggplant
  • 1 potato
  • 1/2 carrot
  • 3 tbsp boxed coconut milk
  • A little bit of tamarind paste
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 shallots
  • 1 stalk curry leaves
  • 4 dried chillies

Instructions for Roti Canai

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, mix the plain flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and add the condensed milk, egg, and water. Mix until a soft dough forms. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Divide the dough into small balls, and coat each with margarine. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 2 hours.
  2. Shape the Roti Canai: On a lightly greased surface, flatten each dough ball with your hands until it becomes thin and round. Fold the edges to form a square shape.
  3. Cook the Roti Canai: Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat. Place the flattened dough on the griddle and cook until bubbles form and the bottom is golden brown, about 2 minutes. Flip and cook the other side until golden brown.
  4. Serve: Serve the Roti Canai warm with curry or any side of your choice.

Instructions for Dalcha Curry

  1. Prepare the Dal: Rinse the mung dal under cold water until the water runs clear. In a pot, add the mung dal, garlic, turmeric powder, and enough water to cover the dal. Cook until the dal is tender, about 20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Curry Base: In a separate pan, heat the cooking oil and add the mustard seeds, cinnamon stick, shallots, curry leaves, and dried chillies. Saut√© until the shallots are golden brown. Add the curry powder and chili powder, and cook for another minute.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the cooked mung dal to the curry base. Stir in the chicken bones, bottle gourd, eggplant, potato, carrot, and coconut milk. Simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 20-25 minutes. Add tamarind paste to taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  4. Serve: Serve the Dalcha Curry hot with Roti Canai or rice.
